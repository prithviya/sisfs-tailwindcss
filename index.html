Frequently Asked Questions

SISFS is a government initiative to provide financial assistance to startups for early-stage funding, covering areas such as proof of concept, prototype development, product trials, market entry, and commercialization.

Startups recognized by DPIIT and incorporated in India less than two years before applying are eligible. Additionally, startups must have a viable business idea in terms of commercialization and social impact.

You can apply by visiting the AIC RAISE website and filling out the application form. After submitting, your proposal will be reviewed, and eligible startups will be selected for funding.

Startups can receive seed funding of up to ₹20 Lakhs for validation of Proof of Concept, prototype development, and product trials, and up to ₹50 Lakhs for market entry and commercialization.

As an Incubation Partner for SISFS, AIC RAISE provides seed funding, mentorship, investor connections, and incubation support to startups to help them grow and secure follow-on funding.

Startups that have already raised funds from angel investors, venture capitalists, or other private equity are not eligible for SISFS.

The scheme prioritizes startups that focus on innovation, development, or improvement of products or processes and have a significant potential for impact.

Yes, startups can apply for multiple schemes, provided they meet the eligibility criteria for each.
